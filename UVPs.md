# H-AGI

**H-AGI is a human-led AI protocol for augmenting human intelligence without outsourcing human judgement. Drawing on the tradition of extended cognition, it treats LLMs as external reasoning tools that can expand working memory, search, perspective-taking and problem-solving — but only when guided by structured thinking modes, meta-epistemic prompts and deliberate use-strategies. The aim is not to let AI think for you, but to help you think better with AI.**

**H-AGI helps people use AI as an extension of human intelligence rather than a replacement for it. Instead of passively asking an LLM for answers, users select thinking modes such as Comprehend, Challenge, Decide, Plan, Probe, Reframe or Learn, then apply structured prompts that keep attention on assumptions, evidence, alternatives, decisions and learning. It protects human intelligence from passive offloading while enhancing it through disciplined AI-assisted reasoning.**

**The risk of AI is not only that machines become more capable, but that humans become more passive, more predictable and more easily routed by systems optimised for productivity, efficiency or stability rather than human autonomy. H-AGI offers a different path: a human-centred protocol for using LLMs as cognitive extensions while keeping judgement, agency, learning and purpose under human control.**

**H-AGI is a human-led protocol for using AI as extended cognition: it augments human intelligence through structured LLM use, while protecting judgement, autonomy and learning through meta-epistemic prompts, thinking modes and deliberate use-strategies.**

**Use AI without being cognitively outsourced: H-AGI turns LLMs into structured extensions of human intelligence, guided by human judgement, agency and learning.**
